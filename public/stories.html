Samuel Njoroge, Master Artisan

From Kibera to Master Craftsman: Samuel's 25-Year Journey

By Amina Wambui October 15, 2023 8 min read

In the narrow alleys of Kibera, a young Samuel Njoroge would watch his grandfather repair leather sandals using nothing but a needle, thread, and decades of wisdom. Today, his hands craft watches that grace wrists from Nairobi to New York.

"I never imagined leatherworking could take me around the world," Samuel reflects, carefully stitching a watch strap. "But here I am, teaching apprentices and seeing my work in magazines."

"The thread doesn't just connect leather—it connects generations. Every stitch honors my grandfather's memory." — Samuel Njoroge

Samuel's journey from a self-taught artisan to SUED's master leatherworker is a testament to Nairobi's creative spirit. His story is woven into every watch he touches, each stitch carrying the resilience and beauty of his community.
